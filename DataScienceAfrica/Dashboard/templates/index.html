About the Model Covid-19 Trends

The Problem

The ubiquity of social media has made it easier to spread misinformation, rumors, and conspiracy theories around the COVID-19 outbreak. There has not been much done to track and evaluate the impact of the COVID-19 and the transmission mitigation strategies put in place to combat its spread as a way of informing Ugandan government policy.

Objective

The main objective of this research is to develop a misinformation classification and detection model in order to analyze and enhance the availability of reliable and trustworthy information around COVID-19. This project will also explore social media data to understand the perceptions and sentiments of people as they relate to the Ugandan government COVID-19 transmission mitigation strategies.

Methods

Social media data from Twitter and Facebook was labeled and used to train an objectivity classifier. The feature engineering process involved extracting features (n-grams, tf-idf, word2vec) from the social media data which were then used to train the baseline models. Machine Learning models with the best performance for the Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis tasks on the test set have been deployed to classify covid-19 related text data which can be displayed on a dashboard. Although social media data is freely available, care was taken not to disclose any personal information, for example, the names of people who post their views on social media platforms. The resultant data set will be published as a Mendeley data under the creative commons license and a data paper has been published.

Outcome

To capture the sentiments and shifts in public sentiments towards the different COVID-19 aspects, and use them to inform public policy on the control of COVID-19.

This project addresses the problem of misinformation that could harm the public by creating fear, increasing panic and angst, threatening the physical safety of individuals and limiting the effectiveness of official efforts to curb the pandemic. It provides a system for identifying misinformation around COVID-19 on social media in Uganda. The dashboard and data mining modules of the project provide a single point for trend analysis and aspect-based sentiment analysis.
