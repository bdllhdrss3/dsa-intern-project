Data Collection

The Data was extracted from Twitter timelines and Facebook pages of the Minister of Health, Major media institutions, Major media personalities and the top 50 Twitter and Facebook users in Uganda.

The Data was filtered using the keywords like ssenyiga ,kolona, corona, virus, obulwadde, corona, covid , abalwadde, ekirwadde, akawuka, staysafeug, stayhome, tonsemberera, tokwatakudereva, vaccine).

The Data collected was in Luganda, English and Codemix of both English and Luganda

Data Annotation

Doccano Tool

This tool (an open source text annotation tool) was used to annotated the data.

The attributes that were used during the annotation process included the Data source (FaceBook, Twitter), Language (English, Luganda, Codemixed), Aspects (truck drivers, hospitals, vaccine, cases, SOPs, NPIs, Testing, Border, Covid19_Impact, Presidential address, death, elections and Covid19), Sentiment (Positive, Negative) and MisInformation (Not Fake, Fake, Partially Fake, and Others).

This data was later reviewed to prevent bias and identify missing annotations

Data Analysis

Most of the annotated data points were from Facebook and were written in English

Representation of Misinformation

Data from Facebook constituted almost 70% of the fake data. The “Others” category contained those comments or post that could not be verified if they are fake or not.
